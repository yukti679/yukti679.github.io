Dance

I have been learning the Indian classical dance form of Bharatanatyam for 11 years. I have performed at various competitions such as the Ekal Dance Deewane and NEMA dance competition. During Hindhu festivals, I also perform at temples and community events. After 7 years of traing, I completed my Salangai Pooja (ankle bells ceremony) in 2021.

Dance has always been a way for me to express myself creatively and connect with my cultural roots. It has taught me discipline, perseverance, and the importance of hard work. Through dance, I have also had the opportunity to meet new people and make lifelong friends who share my passion for this art form.

In the future, I hope to continue learning and growing as a dancer, exploring new styles and techniques, and sharing my love of dance with others. I believe that dance has the power to bring people together, transcend cultural boundaries, and inspire positive change in the world.

Singing

I have been learning the Carnatic form of singing (Sangeetham) for 11 years. I have knowledge in the Sarali Swaras, Janta Swaras, Dattu Swaras, Alankaras, Geethams, Varanams, Swarajathis, Swarapallavis, and Keerthanams. I have also passed the level 1 exam conducted by SAMPADA, which is affiliated with Potti Sri Ramulu Telugu University in India, with distinction.

I have also been a member of my school's chorus for 4 years, where I have had the opportunity to perform at various school events and concerts. These include the winter, spring, and Pops concerts. During the winter and spring concerts we sing more classical pieces and during the Pops concert we sing Pop songs.

Singing has been a way for me to cope with stress during the busy school year. Whenever I feel overwhelmed with schoolwork, I like to take a break and practice singing. It helps me relax and refocus, allowing me to approach my studies with a clearer mind
